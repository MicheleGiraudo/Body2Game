Our Project
We are four students from class 4A Rob at the Technical Industrial Institute “Mario Delpozzo” in Cuneo: Matteo Risso, Michele Giraudo, Mattia Valfrè, and Daniele Revello. We have had the honor and the responsibility of working on this project dedicated to people affected by multiple sclerosis. Our project aims to support people with multiple sclerosis by transforming physical and cognitive rehabilitation exercises into more relaxing, engaging, and effective experiences. We want to approach this topic with the right balance of sensitivity and positivity, in order to reduce stress and improve the quality of life of patients.
The goal of this project is twofold: on one hand, we want to raise public awareness about this complex and often little-understood disease; on the other hand, we aim to develop tools and resources that can concretely improve the daily life of people affected by multiple sclerosis. We have chosen to focus on therapeutic games, because we firmly believe that play can be a powerful means of physical and mental rehabilitation.
Our project includes four games, each carefully selected to balance fun and therapeutic benefits. The chosen games are extremely enjoyable and engaging, with a positive impact both physically and mentally on the participants. Fun is essential to maintain interest and engagement over time, making the experience pleasant and motivating. The games are developed for the specific needs of people with multiple sclerosis, stimulating movement, coordination, strength, and flexibility, thereby improving their physical condition in a fun and relaxed way. Additionally, the games promote mental well-being by reducing stress, increasing focus, and improving mood.
